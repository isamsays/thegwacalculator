How to Calculate GWA — Step-by-step Guide

The General Weighted Average (GWA) reflects your academic performance using a weighted mean of your grades. Understanding the calculation helps you verify results and plan improvement strategies.

Formula

GWA = (Sum of (Grade × Units)) ÷ (Total Units)

Step-by-step example

Imagine you have the following subjects:

  • Calculus — Grade 1.75 — 4 units
  • Physics — Grade 2.00 — 3 units
  • English — Grade 1.50 — 3 units

Weighted sum = (1.75×4) + (2.00×3) + (1.50×3) = 7.0 + 6.0 + 4.5 = 17.5. Total units = 10. GWA = 17.5 ÷ 10 = 1.75.

Tips for accurate calculation

  • Always use official grade values as recorded on your transcript.
  • Include practicum, lab, and elective units when relevant — they affect weighting.
  • Round only when the receiving institution requires a specific presentation (e.g., two decimals).
