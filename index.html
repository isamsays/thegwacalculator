What is GWA?

General Weighted Average (GWA) is a numeric representation of your overall academic performance. It is calculated by multiplying each subject‚Äôs grade by its unit (or credit), summing the results, and dividing by the total number of units.

Think of it as a weighted scorecard: subjects with higher units (like major courses) influence your GWA more than minor ones (like electives or PE).

Common Questions About GWA (FAQ)

Q1: What is the passing GWA?
In most Philippine universities, a GWA of 3.0 or below is considered passing.

Q2: Can I convert my GWA to GPA?
Yes, but conversion scales differ. Check with your university or institution.

Q3: Does PE or NSTP affect GWA?
Yes, all credited subjects with grades are factored in, including PE and NSTP.

Q4: How can I recover if my GWA is low?
Focus on improving in high-unit courses, retake failed subjects (if allowed), and implement better study habits.
