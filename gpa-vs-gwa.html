GPA vs GWA — Understanding the Difference

GPA (Grade Point Average) and GWA (General Weighted Average) are metrics used to summarize student performance. GPA is commonly used internationally and often follows a 4.0 scale, while GWA is commonly used in the Philippines on a 5.0 scale where lower numbers indicate better performance.

Why they look different

GPA centers on a small scale (e.g., 0 to 4.0) where higher is better. GWA typically uses a 1.0 to 5.0 scale where 1.0 is top performance and 5.0 means failure. Conversions between the two require careful mapping to preserve rank and meaning.

Practical conversion approach

Our calculator uses a linear mapping for 4.0 → 5.0 conversions: subtract from 5.0 and rescale. This method preserves distance between grades and provides a consistent proxy when institutions do not provide official equivalence tables.

Always verify with the target school's admissions or registrar office for official conversions used for admissions, scholarships, or transfer credit.
